WHAT IS DST?

Don't Starve Together (DST) is the standalone multiplayer expansion of the uncompromising wilderness survival game, Don't Starve. Now including Reign of Giants; adding new characters, creatures, and Giant new challenges to DST.

Enter a strange and unexplored world full of strange creatures, dangers, and surprises. Gather resources to craft items and structures that match your survival style. Play your way as you unravel the mysteries of this strange land.

Cooperate with your friends in a private game, or take your chances with strangers online!

SAY HELLO TO DST CHARACTERS!

Wilson Percival Higgsbury is the main protagonist of the game. He's a witty scientist wannabe that has a sense of humor. He also has a peculiar haircut resembling the letter "W". Being the default character his Health, Hunger, and Sanity all have default values and he has no drawbacks. Wilson is meant for better Winter survival and efficiency, as well as easier access to Meat Effigies. His stats make him a good all-around character, and many players find that Wilson, as well as his Beard, provides welcome advantages to surviving in the wilderness.

Willow is a young woman who suffers from the impulse control disorder Pyromania, wherein the sufferer feels a constant need to light fires. She is immune to Fire damage, and regenerates Sanity when near Fires. She also comes equipped with Willow's Lighter, which is an infinite source of Light and can be used to light things on Fire. This gives her an easier time when dealing with the dark and low Sanity.

Wolfgang is a simple-minded Strongman who is very boastful about his might. When he has a high amount of Hunger, he moves faster, does more damage, has more Health than most other Characters. But he becomes weaker when his Hunger is low. He becomes scared when in the Dark or near Monsters though, so he loses Sanity more quickly than normal. Overall Wolfgang is a very powerful Character, his drawbacks can be tough to manage for inexperienced players.

Wendy relates everything to death and how depressing life is. Thanks to her personality she loses less Sanity in the Dark or when facing Monsters, giving her an easier time with managing Sanity. She can summon the Ghost of her sister Abigail to assist her in combat. To make up for this, Wendy herself deals less damage in both melee and ranged combat. Abigail gives Wendy a more 'passive' playstyle, letting her sister deal with most threats and staying away from danger.

WX-78 is a self-sustaining automaton that hates all living matter. Being a machine, it takes damage when it is in the Rain unprotected. When struck by Lightning, WX-78 will experience "SYSTEM OVERLOAD", emitting Light while moving at an increased speed. WX-78 can also eat stale and spoiled Food without the negative effects. Overall, WX-78 is a very good character with great bonuses and very small drawbacks.

Wickerbottom is an old, intelligent librarian, an author, and is able to identify many different types of fauna and flora. Wickerbottom starts the game with 2 Papyrus, she is able to craft Science Items with a lower tier Crafting Station than other Characters. She is able to craft Books that give different effects when read in exchange for Sanity. She suffers from insomnia, can eat only fresh Food. Wickerbottom is suited for both early and late game playthroughs.

Woodie is a stereotypical Canadian lumberjack with a love for chopping Trees and camping, who has a mysterious curse. He has Lucy the Axe in his inventory from the start of the game. Due to being a lumberjack, he's very proficient at cutting Trees, doing it faster and with fewer swings than other Characters. When he swings an Axe at a Tree too much or there is a full Moon, Woodie's curse will manifest and he will be turned into a Werebeaver.

Wes is a fragile French mime that uses pantomimes to communicate instead of speaking. He has a maximum of 113 Health and Hunger, and 150 Sanity. His Hunger drains faster than normal and he deals less damage in both melee and ranged combat. Wes is a Balloonomancer, and comes equipped with his Pile o' Balloons. Wes' purpose in the game is to provide a challenge to experienced players.

Maxwell (AKA William Carter) looks very tall and sinister, wears a dapper tuxedo. Players can find Maxwell Statues or Chess Biomes themed after him. He seems to be a part-human, part-demon being. He is the main antagonist of Don't Starve. But in Don't Starve Together, chronologically the events in the game happen after the completion of Adventure Mode in Don't Starve. Because of this, Maxwell is surviving in the world with all the other characters.

Wigfrid although has the appearance of a valkyrie, she's just a stage actress. Wigfrid starts the game with the Battle Spear and Battle Helm, two craftable items that are exclusive to her. The Helm protects for 80% of incoming damage. Both these items prove incredibly useful in the early days of the game, due to their cheap cost and high stats. Wigfrid has a Meat-only diet and is able to eat food items classified as "goodies". When she kills an enemy, she gains Health and Sanity.

Webber looks like a humanoid spider, though he's actually a child that lives inside the spider that tried to eat him long ago. The spider eyes still move at times, suggesting the Spider is still alive. Webber is able to grow a beard made of Silk. Due to his appearance, Spiders will be neutral to him and can be befriended, while Mobs hostile towards Spiders will be hostile towards him too. Webber's playstyle and strength relies on making friends out of normally hostile mobs.

Winona is a factory worker (though also described as an engineer), who dislikes laziness and to a lesser degree, things from high society. Winona starts with 3 Trusty Tapes in her inventory. She takes no damage or sanity loss from the first attack after entering darkness. Also, because she is a skilled builder, she takes half as long as other Characters to craft items.

Warly is probably New Caledonian, judging by his skin tone and French influence. He gets hungry 20% faster than most characters, can only eat food cooked in a crockpot. He has the ability to craft a Chef Pouch, additional Portable Crock Pots, a Portable Seasoning Station, a Portable Grinding Station. The Chef Pouch is usable by any character, but the other items are only usable by Warly.

Wortox is a playful imp with a colourful past. He was once a gentle forest creature without a care in the world - until the day he inherited a terrible curse, and lost a close friend. A Soul is an item exclusive to Wortox. When Mobs or players die, souls will flow to Wortox. He can also spend one Soul to be able to Soul Hop, or teleport, a short distance. Wortox will lose less Sanity when near monsters.

Wormwood's personality is friendly while a little eccentric, addressing things such as insects as "Bzzters" and birds as "Tweeters". He has unique abilities pertaining to Plants and plant-like Mobs, along with access to several crafting recipes. He can plant Seeds without the use of a Farm, and is treated neutrally by Mobs (the Lureplant and Snaptooth Flytrap). He has some sort of kinship with plants, addressing most of them as "Friend," or things similar.

Wurt is a young, curious Merm Guard. She is able to craft the Royal Tapestry, facilitating the creation of the King of the Merms. Wurt can befriend Merms by feeding them, and they can help her fight, chop trees, and mine boulders. She can craft Marsh Turf, which increases her movement speed when walked, can see where Tentacles are hiding. Keeping a living Fish, Eel, Ocean Fishes or Star-Sky in her inventory gives her Sanity.

Walter is an odd, but cheerful boy. The only thing that could ever rival Walter's love for the great outdoors was his fascination with monsters and mystery. Walter has a Trusty Slingshot as an exclusive weapon with special Slingshot Ammo with varying effects. He can also exclusively craft a portable Camper's Tent. Woby is a special Mob that follows him around, she has 9 inventory slots.

Wanda is an enigmatic woman of... well, fluctuating age, she was torn from the world she knew after accidentally creating a fracture in time and space, pulling her from her own timeline into the land of the Constant. Wanda's special power is a Crafting Tab "Clocksmithy". This allows her to craft 7 different character exclusive watches and tools with Time Pieces and other ingredients.
